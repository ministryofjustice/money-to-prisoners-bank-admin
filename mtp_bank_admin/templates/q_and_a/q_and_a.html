Send money to a prisoner service - FAQs


Information for prison switchboard, Zendesk and business hub staff to help answer questions from the public.

1. How can I send money to a prisoner using the new service?

Directly with your debit card.
Or by bank transfer using online banking, a mobile banking app or telephone banking.


2. What bank cards can I use?

You can use Visa, Mastercard and Maestro.


3. What payment methods do not work?

You can not use CHAPS.
And you can not make a payment in-person at a bank using IBAN.


4. Can I send money to a prisoner using this service?

Yes. The digital service (bank transfers and debit cards) is now available to all public prisons in England and Wales.
The service is not available in private prisons.


5. How long will it take for the prisoner to receive the money?

Up to 3 working days.


6. Has the prisoner received my money?

Ask the prisoner when they call you.


7. Why didn’t the money reach the prisoner?

If you paid by debit card, contact your bank.
If you paid by bank transfer, it may be because:


  • the reference you inserted for the bank transfer is wrong - check with the prisoner that you have the prisoner’s correct date of birth and correct prisoner number
  • the prisoner has left prison
  • you paid cash in-branch (not allowed)
  • you made an international transfer (not allowed)

You’ll ONLY get a refund if the reference you inserted for the bank transfer is incorrect OR if the prisoner has left prison.


8. How long will it take for me to get a refund from my bank transfer?

4 working days from the day you sent the money.


9. How will I know I’ve got my refund?

Check your bank account 4 working days after you sent the money.
On your bank statement you’ll see the refund appear as ‘GBS RE NOMS AGENCY’.


10. How do I check the correct reference number for my bank transfer?

Go to: www.gov.uk/send-prisoner-money

Try to generate the reference.
If the reference can’t be generated, check with the prisoner that you have their correct date of birth and correct prisoner number.
Sometimes the date of birth is entered incorrectly into the prison system.
The prisoner might need to ask an officer on the wing to check it and let you know.


11. How do I get my money back if I made a bank transfer in-branch or by international transfer?

You need to talk to the prison business hub staff to identify the payment. When it’s been identified you will need to write to them confirming:


  • who you are
  • your address
  • how you made the payment (in-branch or international transfer)
  • if in-branch, what bank/branch you went to
  • your bank
  • your sort code, account number
  • prisoner’s name
  • prisoner’s prison number

When the prison business hub staff get your letter, they’ll send you a cheque.


12. If I send money using the service how will my data be used or stored?

Go to: www.gov.uk/send-prisoner-money

Click on ‘Terms and Conditions’ at the bottom of the page.
